Basic Life Support Ambulance

For patients who are clinically stable /non critical .

These types of ambulances are used for diagnostic transfers or, transfers of stable patients after discharge. Only basic equipment like BP machine, Pulse Oxymetry, Oxygen delivery devices, AED are available with cabin crew (patient attendant and a nurse).

KEY FEATURES


Advanced Life Support Ambulance

Used to transfer clinically critical patients requiring ventilator support, constant monitoring of ECG/BP/respiratory rate

These ambulances are equipped with Ventilator, Multipara monitors, Defibrillator, Syringe pump and Pacemakers. Doctor and nurses provided on-board.

KEY FEATURES


Neonatal Ambulance

Equipped to transfer critically ill new born child (Neonate-Newly born/less than 30 days of age).

Special equipments like Transport incubator, Neonatal ventilator, etc included to ensure that babies can be moved quickly for the right care in the right place for their needs.

KEY FEATURES


Patient Transport Vehicle

PTV is a non emergency patient transport vehicle equipped with patient bed & other life-support devices.

KEY FEATURES


Mortuary Ambulance

Exclusively used for dead body transfers.

Equipped with freezer to prevent putrefaction/decomposition of dead body.


Air Ambulance

These are dedicated air ambulances designed to transfer critically ill patients requiring ventilator, cardiac monitoring, etc.

Air ambulances contain ventilators, defibrillators, infusion pumps, balloon pumps, oxygen cylinders, trained doctors, & nurses.
